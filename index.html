Biography

Jonathan Tamen is a young changemaker, nonprofit founder, and local STEM education advocate from Miami, Florida. As a high school student, teaming up with his twin brother and several other classmates, Jonathan founded Helping Hands MB to expand access to STEM education for young students, especially from underprivileged backgrounds, in order to improve their academic confidence.

Each year, more than 300 students participate in their projects, such as a free STEM enrichment summer camp offering bilingual instruction and hands-on activities that build academic skills and confidence.

A grandson of Holocaust survivors, Jonathan is inspired by his grandmother’s message in her Shoah Foundation interview to ‘spread kindness’ and hopes to build a better world where no student feels less than.

Now, Jonathan is a graduating senior at Clark University and is attending law school this coming fall, where he plans to pursue a career in education policy and working in the public interest.

Artist Statement

I care a lot about democratizing education. My goal is to create pieces that are useful, inspiring, or at the very least, educational. I try to experiment with the physical form of my pieces while maintaining a clear, and non-fiction-like flow of information.

Across all my pieces, I write about my passions - whether projects I am working on, the community I grew up in, or new things I am trying out. For me, writing sometimes serves as a tool for self-clarification; I often find myself conflicted regarding my passions, asking myself: "Am I doing any good for this person?" or "Do I genuinely care about this thing?” My writing helps me to clarify and remind myself of who I want to be.

Regarding my process, I always start with a blank piece of paper. Even if I am writing code, I will plan it out on paper first. From there, I bounce around and may start on the ending then jump to the beginning then feel inspired to finish the climax. I start with an outline and gradually refine sections at random from there.
