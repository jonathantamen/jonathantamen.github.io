**_Autumn_ – the third season of the year**

The sun was set. Daylight was over. Cold, and I wrapped myself up like a congregation huddling close to raise their hands together in prayer. I am just a young prayer plant on sale for £7 at John Watkins Plaza in front of the library at the London School of Economics.

I was a beautiful green. Like the skin of an underripe lime, its darkest shade of green. Underneath my leaves and on my stem, I had undertones of deep, dark purple adding depth to my appearance. My stripes were everywhere on me and a very pale green – almost white. I was healthy and so was Jonathan. He named me "Spots" after my stripes.

We walk home north on Kingsway, through the heart of London, one of its busiest streets. He did not talk to me the whole way. Finally, in his humble room inside Connaught Hall, a 100-year-old building, new by British standards, he shows me my place. A nice, classic wooden windowsill facing toward the sunrise – just like I like it.

**_Winter_ – the coldest season of the year**

Winter is a tough season for me because the short days mean I get to enjoy less sunlight. My family is originally from Brazil and I love sunny weather.[^1] The greenhouse outside of London where I spent most of my life was a sad step down.

The glass window where I now contemplate every day alone is not better. The thin material and tiny gaps in the cracks of the wooden frame and sealing let chilly air inside.

I could tell the winter was tough on Jonathan and he could see it in me too. My leaves were losing some of their color. His skin was losing its life. Most days were the same monotonous routine: he would wake up before me, before the sunrise, to commute to school; I would try my best to holdout through the dreary weather; after dark, we would do nothing and go to sleep.

On a day that broke our rhythm, he paid attention to me beyond the bare requirements to sustain my life. I got to be a model while Jonathan drew me. The drawing did not resemble me at all.

**_Spring_ – the season before summer**

Today, we reunited after a month apart for 'winter break.' My leaves are still not what they used to be. Winter took a toll on me. Jonathan seems marginally more motivated. He notices my appearance and comments on it. With understanding he says to me "I know winter is hard. But I am gonna keep trying as long as you are trying."

My average day in Spring is not much different than last season but at least Jonathan places me outside on the warm days and talks to me about his feelings. He misunderstands what plant therapy is.

**_Summer_ – final exam season**

While warm days became common, attention from Jonathan became less so. Until one day he told me with guilt that I could not come with him to the States, his home. Instead, I am left to suffocate in the bottom of a rubbish bin.
