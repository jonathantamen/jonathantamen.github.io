**Wake up and trek to the beach to watch the sun rise.** My transportation method of choice is to bike. But walking is also good. According to some doctors, being up with the sunrise each day is one of the healthiest ways to start your morning. It is also one of my favorites. Since you are east, North Shore Park is only a 10-minute bike ride away from the boardwalk, so you can officially start the challenge.

**Once at the park, stop first at the dog section.** No matter the time of day – weather dependent – there will be people playing with their pets. Divided into two sections for big and small, you may be able to play with someone's dog. At worst, you can watch them sniff, jump, run, and bark from the paved path off to the side of the dog fence. Try to ignore the crude behavior of some of the males that happens on occasion when their owner is not paying attention.

**Follow along the footpath's guided workout routine.** There are accessible exercise machines for all body types and muscle groups. If you find the group of ladies who practice yoga every morning (I think) from 8am, feel free to join them. They are a nice group. If you find the couple practicing Zumba, do not. You will not be able to keep up.

**Enjoy a cookout.** Perhaps what the park is best known for by its surrounding immigrant, mostly Hispanic community is its excellence as a barbecuing spot. My family has enjoyed a cookout together in years past and yours can too. However, we are often less fond of certain parties who blast unpopular music that drowns out the sounds of the whistling winds and crashing ocean waves. But if you listen closely, you can still hear the occasional squawks from a pandemonium of parakeets overhead.

**For children, enjoy the playground, probably while the food is cooking.** In 2023, the City of Miami Beach remodeled the playground with improved accessibility, more lighting, and repaved concrete paths.[^1]

**Lay in the grass for stargazing.** It is not the softest grass nor are there many stars to see but you can still try. You will have the best luck out of any park in Miami Beach due to the least amount of light pollution from nearby buildings.

**Lastly, take care of our city and it will take care of you.** So, do not feed the iguanas, they are invasive, and the wildlife will be grateful to you. Do not litter and your view will be more pristine. Do not smoke and you will better smell the salty air.

**Note:** Weather independent, there will always be others with the idea to celebrate a birthday, holiday, or nice day, but the park spans almost 28 acres,[^2] so you can find a secluded spot in the shade of a Seagrape tree to enjoy your time under. Their dinner-plate size leaves shake back and forth in the wind for you like Salsa dancers.
