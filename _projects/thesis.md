description: "This paper seeks to define ‘resilient financial practices’ for the US non-profitsector to follow that are correlated with helping non-profits to do more when the economy doesworse. This paper utilizes Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) panel data regressions with fixedeffects (year, state, industry, and firm), alongside a probit regression model on 2.88 millionobservations of 501(c)(3) tax exempt organizations from 2009-2023. The results confirm thenon-profit sector remains largely procyclical. However, maintaining highly liquid, unrestrictedrevenue streams or pre-existing pipelines for government grants is correlated with increases in anorganization’s probability to expand when society needs them most."
