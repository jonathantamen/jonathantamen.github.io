description: "My undergraduate thesis seeks to define ‘resilient financial practices’ for the US non-profit sector that may help them to do more when the economy does worse. This paper utilizes Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) panel data regressions with fixed effects (year, state, industry, and firm), alongside a probit regression model on 2.88 million observations of 501(c)(3) tax exempt organizations from 2009-2023. The results confirm the non-profit sector remains largely procyclical. However, maintaining highly liquid, unrestricted revenue streams or pre-existing pipelines for government grants is correlated with increases in an organization’s probability to expand when society needs them most."
